Extra Info

Characteristic Description
Definition A large city, typically one with a population of over one million
Examples New York City, Tokyo, London
History The concept of a metropolis dates back to ancient civilizations, such as Greece and Rome. The term itself comes from the Greek words "metro," meaning mother, and "polis," meaning city.
Attributes Metropolises often have a diverse population, a central business district, and significant cultural and economic influence.
Famous Landmarks Statue of Liberty, Eiffel Tower, Big Ben, Times Square, Tokyo Tower
